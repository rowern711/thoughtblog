Prayer is often imagined as a private whisper, yet it spills over into the way we vote, volunteer, and greet our neighbors. The interior life writes the script for the exterior one.

When a tradition asks us to love the stranger, it is training our attention. The person in front of us becomes a reminder that the divine refuses to stay contained by our categories.
